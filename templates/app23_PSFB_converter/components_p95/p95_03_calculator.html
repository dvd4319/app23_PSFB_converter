2. Power Transformer Turns Ratio Calculation

$$ V_o = V_{in} \cdot \frac{N_s}{N_p} \cdot D \Rightarrow \frac{N_p}{N_s} = \frac{V_{in}}{V_o} \cdot D $$ $$ \frac{N_p}{N_s} = \frac{V_{in, \min}}{V_{o, \max}} \cdot D_{\max}= {{ results.N_ratio_max}} $$

3. Derivation of Ideal and Effective Duty Cycle

$$ D = \frac{V_o}{V_{in}} \cdot \frac{N_p}{N_s} = {{ results.D_ideal}} $$

3.1. Voltage drop across the leakage inductance

$$ \delta V_{L_k} = L_k \cdot I_o \cdot \frac{N_s}{N_p} \cdot F_{sw}= {{results.delta_V_Lk}} V $$

3.2. Voltage drop due to the resistance of the transformer windings

$$ \delta V_{TR} = I_o \cdot \left( \frac{N_s}{N_p} \cdot R_{prim} + \frac{N_p}{N_s} \cdot R_{sec} \right) = {{results.delta_V_TR}} V $$

3.3. Voltage drop across the switching semiconductor elements (MOSFET)

$$ \delta V_{R_{dson}} = I_o \cdot \left( \frac{N_s}{N_p} \cdot 2 \cdot R_{dson_{PR}} + \frac{N_p}{N_s} \cdot R_{dson_{SR}} \right) = {{results.delta_V_Rdson}} V $$

3.4. Voltage drop across the series resistance

$$ \delta V_o = \frac{I_o}{2} \cdot R_{L_o} = {{results.delta_V_Lo}} V $$

3.5. Effective duty cycle ratio

$$ D_{eff} = \frac{V_o + \delta V_o}{V_{in} - \delta V_{L_k} - \delta V_{TR} - \delta V_{R_{dson}}} \cdot \frac{N_p}{N_s}= {{results.D_eff}} $$

3.6. Theoretical maximum effective duty cycle ratio (worst-case scenario)

$$ D_{eff,\max} = \frac{V_{o,\max} + \delta V_o}{V_{in,\min} - \delta V_{L_k} - \delta V_{TR} - \delta V_{R_{dson}}} \cdot \frac{N_p}{N_s} = {{results.D_eff_max}} $$

4. Output Inductor Design

4.1. Output Inductor Value Calculation

4.1.1. Minimum nominal current

$$ I_{o,\min} = (10 \between 30)\ \% \cdot I_o = {{results.Io_min_calc}} A $$

4.1.2. Output inductor ripple

$$ \Delta i_{L_o} = 2 \cdot I_{o,\min} = {{results.delta_I_Lo}} A $$

4.1.3. Output inductor

$$ L_o = \frac{V_{in} \cdot (N_s / N_p) \cdot D \cdot (1-D)}{\Delta i_{L_o} \cdot F_{sw}} = {{results.Lo_uH}} uH $$

4.2. Peak and RMS Inductor Current Calculation

The maximum values $I_{L_o, \max}$ and minimum values $I_{L_o, \min}$ of the current through the output inductors: $$ I_{L_o, \max} = \frac{I_o}{2} + \frac{\Delta i_{L_o}}{2} = {{results.I_Lo_max}} uH $$ $$ I_{L_o, \min} = \frac{I_o}{2} - \frac{\Delta i_{L_o}}{2} = {{results.I_Lo_min}} uH $$

4.2.1. RMS current through the output inductors

$$ I_{L_o, rms} = \sqrt{\frac{I_{L_o, \min}^2 + I_{L_o, \max}^2 + I_{L_o, \min} \cdot I_{L_o, \max}}{3}} = {{results.I_Lo_RMS}} A $$

4.3. Magnetic Core Selection and Maximum Flux Density Calculation

Material: $3C90$ Maximum flux density in the magnetic core: $$ B_{\max} = \frac{L_o \cdot I_{L_o, \max}}{N_{L_o} \cdot A_c} = {{results.B_max_T}} T $$
  1. $N_{L_o}$ - number of inductor turns
  2. $I_{L_o, \max}$ - peak current
  3. $A_c$ - effective cross-sectional area of the magnetic core
$$ A_c = \frac{L_o \cdot I_{L_o, \max}}{N_{L_o} \cdot B_{\max}} = {{results.Ac_ferita_mm2}} cm^2 $$ $$ A_{c_{\min}} = \frac{L_o \cdot I_{L_o, \max}}{N_{L_o} \cdot B_{sat}} = {{results.Ac_min_mm2}} cm^2 $$

5. Secondary-Side Power Switch Design

5.1. Maximum and RMS Current Calculation in the Secondary Circuit

During the conduction period [0, DT]

$$ \Delta i_{L_1} = \frac{V_{in} \cdot \frac{N_s}{N_p} - V_o}{L_o} \cdot \frac{D}{F_{sw}} = {{results.delta_i_L10}} A $$ $$ \Delta i_{L_2} = -\frac{V_o}{L_o} \cdot \frac{D}{F_{sw}} = {{results.delta_i_L20}} A $$ $$ \Delta i_{SR} = \Delta i_{L_1} + \Delta i_{L_2} = {{results.delta_i_SR0}} A $$ $$ I_{SR, \min} = I_o - \frac{\Delta i_{SR}}{2} = {{results.I_SR_min0}} A $$ $$ I_{SR, \max} = I_o + \frac{\Delta i_{SR}}{2} = {{results.I_SR_max0}} A $$ $$ I_{SR, RMS, ON} = \sqrt{\frac{I_{SR, \min}^2 + I_{SR, \max}^2 + I_{SR, \min} \cdot I_{SR, \max}}{3}} = {{results.I_SR_RMS_ON}} A $$

During the conduction period [0, (0.5-D)T]

$$ \Delta i_{L_1} = \Delta i_{L_2} = \frac{V_o}{2 \cdot L_o} \cdot \frac{0.5-D}{F_{sw}} = {{results.delta_i_L11}} A $$ $$ \Delta i_{SR} = \Delta i_{L_1} + \Delta i_{L_2} = {{results.delta_i_L21}} A $$ $$ I_{SR, \min} = I_o - \frac{\Delta i_{SR}}{2} = {{results.I_SR_min1}} A $$ $$ I_{SR, \max} = I_o + \frac{\Delta i_{SR}}{2} = {{results.I_SR_max1}} A $$ $$ I_{SR, RMS, FW} = \sqrt{\frac{I_{SR, \min}^2 + I_{SR, \max}^2 + I_{SR, \min} \cdot I_{ SR, \max }}{3}} = {{results.I_SR_RMS_FW}} A $$ $$ I_{SR,RMS} = \sqrt{D \cdot I_{SR,RMS,ON}^2 + (0.5 - D) \cdot I_{SR,RMS, FW}^2} = {{results.I_SR_RMS}} A $$

6. Primary-Side Power Switch Design

Current Calculation Through Primary-Side Power Switches

Magnetizing current: $$ I_m = \frac{V_{in}}{L_p} \cdot \frac{D}{F_{sw}} = {{results.Im}} A $$

During the conduction interval [0,DT]

Primary current (minimum and maximum): $$ I_{p_{\min}} = I_{L_{o,\min}} \cdot \frac{N_s}{N_p} - \frac{I_m}{2} = {{results.I_p_min}} A $$ $$ I_{p_{\max}} = I_{L_{o,\max}} \cdot \frac{N_s}{N_p} + \frac{I_m}{2} = {{ results.I_p_max}} A $$
  1. $I_{L_{o,\min}}$ - minimum current through the output inductor
  2. $I_{L_{o,\max}}$ - maximum current through the output inductor
RMS value of the current during the interval [0,DT]: $$ I_{p_{rms,ON}} = \sqrt{\frac{I_{p_{\min}}^2 + I_{p_{\max}}^2 + I_{p_{\min}} \cdot I_{p_{\max}}}{3}} = {{ results.I_p_rms_on}} A $$

During the freewheeling interval [0,(0.5-D)T]

$$ I_{p_{min},2} = I_{p_{\max}} \cdot e^{-\frac{R_e}{L_k} \cdot (0.5-D) \cdot T} + \frac{I_m}{2} = {{ results.I_p_min_2}} A $$
  1. $$L_k$$ - leakage inductance
  2. $$R_e$$ - sum of equivalent resistances referred to the primary side
$$ R_e = 2 \cdot R_{dson_{PR}} + R_{prim} + \left( R_{sec} + 2 \cdot R_{dson_{SR}} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{N_p}{N_s} \right)^2 = {{ results.Re_mOhm}} m \Omega $$ RMS value of the current during the freewheeling interval [0,(0.5-D)T]: $$ I_{p_{rms,FW}} = \sqrt{\frac{I_{p_{\min},2}^2 + I_{p_{\max}}^2 + I_{p_{\min},2} \cdot I_{p_{\max}}}{3}} = {{ results.I_p_rms_FW}} A $$ RMS current value through the primary-side power static switches: $$ I_{PR,RMS} = \sqrt{D \cdot I_{p_{rms,ON}}^2 + (0.5 - D) \cdot I_{p_{rms,FW}}^2} = {{ results.I_PR_RMS}} A $$ RMS current in the primary winding of the transformer (2 half-cycles during one switching period): $$ I_{p_{RMS}} = \sqrt{I_{p_{rms,ON}}^2 + I_{p_{rms,FW}}^2} = {{ results.I_p_RMS}} A $$

7. Available Energy Calculation in Leakage Inductance

Energy stored in the parasitic capacitances on the primary side: $$ E_{cap} = \frac{1}{2} \left( 2 \cdot C_{oss} + C_{tr} \right) \cdot V_{in, \max}^2 = {{ results.E_cap_uJ}} \mu C $$
  1. $$C_{oss}$$ - drain-source parasitic capacitance
  2. $$C_{tr}$$ - estimated parasitic capacitance of the transformer
  3. $$V_{in, \max}$$ - maximum input voltage of the converter
Energy stored in the leakage inductance: $$ E_k = \frac{1}{2} \cdot L_k \cdot I_{p_{\min},2}^2 = {{ results.E_k_uJ}} \mu C $$

8. Output Capacitor Calculation

Maximum ripple of the current through the output capacitor: $$ \delta I_{C_o} = \Delta i_{L_1} + \Delta i_{L_2} = {{ results.delta_I_Co}} A $$ Minimum required capacitance: $$ C_{o, \min} = \frac{\delta I_{C_o}}{8 \cdot \Delta V_{s} \cdot F_{sw} \cdot 2} = {{ results.Co_min_uF}} \mu F $$

Converter Loss Analysis

9. Output Inductor Losses

Power losses in the output inductors: $$ P_{L_o} = P_{DCR,L_o} + P_{core,L_o} = {{ results.P_Lo}} W $$ Power dissipated due to the DC winding resistance of the inductor: $$ P_{DCR, L_o} = I_{L_o, rms}^2 \cdot R_{L_o} = {{ results.P_DCR_Lo}} $$ Modified Steinmetz Equation: $$ P_{MSE} = C_m \cdot F_{eq}^x \hat{B}^y = {{ results.P_MSE_mW_cm3}} \frac{m W}{ cm^3 } $$ AC component of the flux density in the magnetic core: $$ \Delta B_{L_o, ac} = \frac{V_{in} \cdot \frac{N_s}{N_p} - V_o}{N_{L_o} A_c} \cdot \frac{D}{F_{sw}} = {{ results.delta_B_ac_T}} ~ T $$ Equivalent frequency for arbitrary (non-sinusoidal) current waveforms (rectangular, PWM): $$ f_{eq} = \frac{2}{\pi^2} \cdot F_{sw} \cdot \frac{1}{D \cdot (1-D)} = {{ results.f_eq_kHz}} ~ kHz $$ Specific losses per volume: $$ P_{MSE} = C_m \cdot f_{eq}^2 \frac{\Delta B_{L_o,ac}^y}{2} \cdot \frac{mW}{cm^3} = {{ results.P_MSE_mW_cm3}} ~ \frac{m W}{ cm^3 } $$ Power loss in the magnetic core: $$ P_{core,L_o} = P_{MSE} \cdot V_c = {{ results.P_core_Lo}} ~ W $$

Primary-Side Power Switch Losses

Primary-side power static switches: $$ P_{PR} = P_{cond, PR} + P_{sw,PR} = {{ results.P_PR}} ~ W $$ Conduction losses on one primary-side power static switch: $$ P_{cond, PR} = I_{PR,RMS}^2 \cdot R_{dson,PR} = {{ results.P_cond_PR}} ~ W $$ Switching losses: $$ P_{sw,PR} = \frac{1}{8} \cdot \left( 2 \cdot C_{oss} + C_{tr} \right) \cdot V_{in}^2 \cdot F_{sw} = {{ results.P_sw_PR}} ~ W $$

Secondary-Side Power Switch Losses

Conduction losses: $$ P_{cond, SR} = I_{SR, RMS}^2 \cdot R_{dson, SR} = {{ results.P_cond_SR}} $$ Switching power loss: $$ P_{sw,SR} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot C_{oss,SR} \cdot \left( V_{in} \cdot \frac{N_s}{N_p} \right) \cdot F_{sw} = {{ results.P_sw_SR}} $$ Total losses for one rectifier branch: $$ P_{SR} = P_{cond, SR} + 2 \cdot P_{sw,SR} = {{ results.P_SR}} $$

Power Transformer Losses

Power dissipated in the primary winding resistance $R_{prim}$: $$ P_{t_{res,prim}} = I_{p_{RMS}}^2 \cdot R_{prim} = {{ results.Pt_res_prim}} $$ Losses due to the secondary winding resistance $R_{sec}$: $$ P_{t_{res,sec}} = I_{L_o, rms}^2 \cdot R_{sec} = {{ results.Pt_res_sec}} $$ Peak flux density in the magnetic core: $$ \hat{B} = \frac{V_{in} \cdot D}{2 \cdot N_p \cdot A_e \cdot F_{sw}} = {{ results.B_hat_T}} $$ Specific losses (per volume): $$ P_{MSE} = C_m \cdot f_{eq}^2 \frac{\Delta B_{L_o,ac}^y}{2} \cdot \frac{mW}{cm^3} = {{ results.P_MSE_tr_mW}} $$ Power losses in the transformer magnetic core: $$ P_{t_{core}} = P_{MSE} \cdot V_c = {{ results.Pt_core}} $$ Total transformer losses: $$ P_t = P_{t_{res,prim}} + P_{t_{res,sec}} + P_{t_{core}} = {{ results.Pt}} $$

Control Circuit Power Consumption

$$ P_{DRV} = V_{cc} \left( 4 \cdot Q_{g_{PR}} + 4 \cdot Q_{g_{SR}} \right) \cdot F_{sw} = {{ results.P_DRV}} $$

Losses Due to Voltage Stress Limiting Circuits

$$ P_{snubber} = 2 \cdot C_s \cdot \left( V_{in} \cdot \frac{N_s}{N_p} \right)^2 \cdot F_{sw} = {{ results.P_snubber}} $$

Loss Breakdown and Efficiency Calculation
