This page includes two complementary sections for designing a boost converter based on typical XL6019-module specifications (Vin: 3-35 V, Vout: 5-40 V, Iin_max = 5 A, Pout_max = 30 W).

  • Interactive Calculator: Automatically computes the minimum inductor (L) and output capacitor (C) using the worst-case critical conditions for a boost topology:
    • For L: the most stressful point occurs at minimum input voltage and maximum output power, where the duty cycle is highest and the inductor current peaks.
    • For C: the worst case is at maximum output voltage and maximum load, where the output ripple is most difficult to control.
    This conservative approach ensures stable operation and acceptable ripple across all valid operating points within the specified range.
  • Manual Example: Provides a detailed step-by-step calculation for a specific high-power operating point (e.g., Vin ≈ 7-12 V, Vout ≈ 31-35 V). At these voltages the duty cycle is moderate, resulting in a larger required inductance (≈ 25-35 µH) compared to the extreme low-Vin case.

Why the difference? In boost converters with wide input/output ranges, the inductor value is always a design compromise. The automatic calculator targets the absolute worst-case (low Vin, high Pout), while the manual example focuses on a realistic high-power use case where efficiency and thermal performance are more favorable. Commercial XL6019 modules typically use inductors in the 22-47 µH range as a practical balance between ripple, size, and saturation limits.

Both approaches are valid and highlight different aspects of boost converter design trade-offs.
