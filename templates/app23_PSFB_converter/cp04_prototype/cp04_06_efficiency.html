This dual-module operation is crucial for meeting efficiency targets, especially at currents below the nominal rating of a single module.

Another key observation is the dependence of efficiency on the input voltage. Analysis shows that the maximum efficiency of 93.5% is reached at the minimum input voltage and an output current of 70A. A secondary efficiency peak of approximately 93% occurs around an output current of 140A. Beyond this point, the efficiency gradually decreases, reaching about 91.5% in the worst-case scenario, at nominal current and maximum input voltage.
