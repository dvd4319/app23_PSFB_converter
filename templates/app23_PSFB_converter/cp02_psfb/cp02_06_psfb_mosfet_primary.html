6. Primary-Side Power Switch Design

Current Calculation Through Primary-Side Power Switches

Magnetizing current: $$ I_m = \frac{V_{in}}{L_p} \cdot \frac{D}{F_{sw}} = {{results.Im}} A $$

During the conduction interval [0,DT]

Primary current (minimum and maximum): $$ I_{p_{\min}} = I_{L_{o,\min}} \cdot \frac{N_s}{N_p} - \frac{I_m}{2} = {{results.I_p_min}} A $$ $$ I_{p_{\max}} = I_{L_{o,\max}} \cdot \frac{N_s}{N_p} + \frac{I_m}{2} = {{ results.I_p_max}} A $$
  1. $I_{L_{o,\min}}$ - minimum current through the output inductor
  2. $I_{L_{o,\max}}$ - maximum current through the output inductor
RMS value of the current during the interval [0,DT]: $$ I_{p_{rms,ON}} = \sqrt{\frac{I_{p_{\min}}^2 + I_{p_{\max}}^2 + I_{p_{\min}} \cdot I_{p_{\max}}}{3}} = {{ results.I_p_rms_on}} A $$

During the freewheeling interval [0,(0.5-D)T]

$$ I_{p_{min},2} = I_{p_{\max}} \cdot e^{-\frac{R_e}{L_k} \cdot (0.5-D) \cdot T} + \frac{I_m}{2} = {{ results.I_p_min_2}} A $$
  1. $L_k$ - leakage inductance
  2. $R_e$ - sum of equivalent resistances referred to the primary side
$$ R_e = 2 \cdot R_{dson_{PR}} + R_{prim} + \left( R_{sec} + 2 \cdot R_{dson_{SR}} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{N_p}{N_s} \right)^2 = {{ results.Re_mOhm}} m \Omega $$ RMS value of the current during the freewheeling interval [0,(0.5-D)T]: $$ I_{p_{rms,FW}} = \sqrt{\frac{I_{p_{\min},2}^2 + I_{p_{\max}}^2 + I_{p_{\min},2} \cdot I_{p_{\max}}}{3}} = {{ results.I_p_rms_FW}} A $$ RMS current value through the primary-side power static switches: $$ I_{PR,RMS} = \sqrt{D \cdot I_{p_{rms,ON}}^2 + (0.5 - D) \cdot I_{p_{rms,FW}}^2} = {{ results.I_PR_RMS}} A $$ RMS current in the primary winding of the transformer (2 half-cycles during one switching period): $$ I_{p_{RMS}} = \sqrt{I_{p_{rms,ON}}^2 + I_{p_{rms,FW}}^2} = {{ results.I_p_RMS}} A $$
