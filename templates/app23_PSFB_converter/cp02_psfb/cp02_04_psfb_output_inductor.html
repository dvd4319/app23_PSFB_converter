4. Output Inductor Design

4.1. Output Inductor Value Calculation

4.1.1. Minimum nominal current

$$ I_{o,\min} = (10 \between 30)\ \% \cdot I_o = {{results.Io_min_calc}} A $$

4.1.2. Output inductor ripple

$$ \Delta i_{L_o} = 2 \cdot I_{o,\min} = {{results.delta_I_Lo}} A $$

4.1.3. Output inductor

$$ L_o = \frac{V_{in} \cdot (N_s / N_p) \cdot D \cdot (1-D)}{\Delta i_{L_o} \cdot F_{sw}} = {{results.Lo_uH}} uH $$

4.2. Peak and RMS Inductor Current Calculation

The maximum values $I_{L_o, \max}$ and minimum values $I_{L_o, \min}$ of the current through the output inductors: $$ I_{L_o, \max} = \frac{I_o}{2} + \frac{\Delta i_{L_o}}{2} = {{results.I_Lo_max}} A $$ $$ I_{L_o, \min} = \frac{I_o}{2} - \frac{\Delta i_{L_o}}{2} = {{results.I_Lo_min}} A $$

4.2.1. RMS current through the output inductors

$$ I_{L_o, rms} = \sqrt{\frac{I_{L_o, \min}^2 + I_{L_o, \max}^2 + I_{L_o, \min} \cdot I_{L_o, \max}}{3}} = {{results.I_Lo_RMS}} A $$

4.3. Magnetic Core Selection and Maximum Flux Density Calculation

Material: $3C90$ Maximum flux density in the magnetic core: $$ B_{\max} = \frac{L_o \cdot I_{L_o, \max}}{N_{L_o} \cdot A_c} = {{results.B_max_T}} T $$
  1. $N_{L_o}$ - number of inductor turns
  2. $I_{L_o, \max}$ - peak current
  3. $A_c$ - effective cross-sectional area of the magnetic core
$$ A_c = \frac{L_o \cdot I_{L_o, \max}}{N_{L_o} \cdot B_{\max}} = {{results.Ac_ferita_mm2}} cm^2 $$ $$ A_{c_{\min}} = \frac{L_o \cdot I_{L_o, \max}}{N_{L_o} \cdot B_{sat}} = {{results.Ac_min_mm2}} cm^2 $$
